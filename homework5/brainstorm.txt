	2. Gravitational Lensing https://science.nasa.gov/mission/hubble/science/science-behind-the-discoveries/hubble-gravitational-lenses/
	In this article, they introduced the concept of gravitational lensing, where light bends, distorts, and magnifies as it passes around a massive object. Today, gravitational lensing is used in the Hubble Space Telescope mission, where for the first time, they measured the mass of a lone white dwarf by seeing how much its gravity deflected the light from a background star. 
	3. Redshift https://www.esa.int/Science_Exploration/Space_Science/What_is_red_shift
	In this article, they introduced the "red shift", where it can be understood as when the wavelength of the light is stretched, the light is shed as "shifted" towards the red part of the spectrum. Since light behaves like a wave, it undergoes a Doppler-like shift if the source is moving relative to us. When a galaxy is moving away from use (universe expansion), light from the galaxies is shifted to longer wavelengths, where it gets redder, understood as "red-shifted".
